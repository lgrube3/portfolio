LOVRNET provides services for low vision patients. Scroll down to read more about my involvement.

Background

Low vision refers to chronic disabling visual impairments that cannot be corrected with eyeglasses or with medical or surgical treatment. The most recent estimates indicate that between 4 and 5 million Americans presently have low vision and that number is expected to double over the next two decades.

LOVRNET addresses this concern as a community-based healthcare network, governed and operated by local Lions, that is dedicated to the expansion, improvement, and use of local low vision rehabilitation services.

My involvement

I was consulted to design and build the LOVRNET system so understanding the current state of low vision rehabilitation was crucial. I also met with the project leaders to discuss the project goals, business model, and technical features.

Following my research and these meetings, I created design concepts and user flow diagrams visually depicting all requirements. The visual design was created using a mix of low vision design best practices and traditional design principles. Intuitive system functions were fundamental in these efforts.

Drupal developers were responsible for interfacing with me to build the functionality. I used my HTML/CSS expertise to create pixel perfect views.
